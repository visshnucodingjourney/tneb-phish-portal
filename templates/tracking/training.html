What happened
You received a fake email, clicked a suspicious link, and entered your login credentials on a page that looked legitimate but was not. This is exactly how real phishing attacks steal accounts.
How to spot phishing emails — remember these:
🔍 Check the sender's email address carefully. Attackers use domains that look similar (e.g. company-portal.net instead of company.com).
🔗 Hover over links before clicking. The real URL appears at the bottom of your browser — check if it matches the company's actual domain.
⚡ Be suspicious of urgency. "Your account will be locked!", "Act now!" — real IT teams don't pressure you like this.
🔒 Never enter credentials on a page you reached via email. Always type the URL directly into your browser.
📞 When in doubt, call IT directly. Never reply to the suspicious email — contact your IT/security team through official channels.
